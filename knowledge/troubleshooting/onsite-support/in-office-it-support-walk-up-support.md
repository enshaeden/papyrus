# **Purpose**

This SOP outlines the procedures for providing in-office IT support via walk-up service to employees. It aims to ensure consistency and efficiency in providing support to minimize downtime and maximize employee satisfaction.

# **Scope**

This SOP applies to all IT support personnel responsible for providing in-office technical assistance to employees (at <OFFICE_SITE_C>, <OFFICE_SITE_A>, <OFFICE_SITE_D>, <OFFICE_SITE_B>) who visit the IT support desk.

# **Definitions**

- **Walk-up Support:** IT support provided to employees who physically visit the IT helpdesk or designated support location.
- **Ticket:** A record of a support request, generated either by the user or the IT support staff.
- **SLA (Service Level Agreement):** A defined agreement outlining the expected response and resolution times for different types of issues.
- **Escalation:** The process of transferring a support request to a higher level of expertise when the initial support staff cannot resolve the issue

# **Procedure**

- **Greetings and Initial Assessment** **-** Acknowledge the employee with a professional and friendly greeting. - Inquire about the issue and ask relevant questions to determine the nature of the problem.
- **Ticket Creation** **-** Request the employee (User) to create a ticket in the IT support system, with a detailed description of the issue.
- **Troubleshooting and Resolution** **-** Perform basic troubleshooting (e.g., restarting the device, checking cables, software diagnostics, network troubleshooting, AV / conference room issues, Activity monitor for slow performance). - If the issue is resolved on the spot, update the ticket with resolution details and close it. - If further work is required:
  - Inform the employee about the next steps and expected resolution timeline.
- **Documentation** **-** Ticket Updates: Document all actions taken, troubleshooting steps performed, and resolutions found within the ticket. - Knowledge Base: If a new solution is discovered, document it in the IT knowledge base for future reference.
- **Closure** **-** Once the issue is resolved, verify with the user that the problem is fixed and that they are satisfied with the solution.

# **Escalation Procedure**

- If the issue cannot be resolved at the walk-up desk - Reach out to IT Leadership or Systems Engineering for help and further support.
- Provide a detailed summary of troubleshooting steps taken before escalation.
- Notify the employee about the escalation and expected resolution timeframe.

# **SLA (Service Level Agreement)**

- **Response Time:** Acknowledge walk-up requests as soon as possible. (Immediate)
- **Resolution Time:** Aim to resolve most of the walk-up issues within 30 minutes - 1 hour. More complex issues may require escalation and longer resolution times.
- **Communication:** Provide regular updates to the user, especially for escalated issues.

# **Review and Continuous Improvement**

- This SOP will be reviewed and updated at least quarterly or as needed to reflect changes in technology, processes, or business requirements.
- Periodically review walk-up support tickets to identify common issues and trends and document them for knowledge-sharing.
