# Room Introduction

### **Available Equipment:**

- Two Panasonic projectors and two retractable screens
  - 1 set serves as overflow in back of room
- Permanent laptop configured with room settings on podium
- 2 TV’s mounted on pillar
  - 1 facing presenter and 1 facing back of room
- 2 touch control panels
  - One on pillar and one near overflow projector
    - Additional I/O for room located underneath pillar
- 3 Shure microphones in charging cradle
  - 1 lapel mic and 2 handheld
- Logitech camera
  - Mounted overhead
- In-ceiling speakers for amplified audio

# Using the Space

1. Log in to podium computer with <COMPANY_NAME> account and the password posted on device
2. If screen and projector do not deploy automatically, locate blue pillar that has touch panel and TV’s mounted
  1. Tap touch panel once to wake and again to Start the System
3. Open <VIDEO_CONFERENCING_PLATFORM> client and click Join Meeting then entering meeting info
  1. Enter desired name when joining
    2. If private meeting, someone invited to <VIDEO_CONFERENCING_PLATFORM> meeting can sign in to their <VIDEO_CONFERENCING_PLATFORM> account in client.
4. If joining <COLLABORATION_PLATFORM> meeting or other, follow join options from invite
5. If using camera, switch to Video to Logitech camera in client
  1. Camera can be controlled with Logitech remote on podium
    2. Camera is very awkwardly placed and can vibrate with HVAC
6. Once meeting is connected, test audio from desired devices
7. Touch panels can be used for the following settings
  1. Turning on Overflow (enabled back projector and screen)
    2. Toggle screens up/down and projectors on/off
      1. If sharing screen from podium computer, projector and screen will auto-deploy after 5 minutes even if turned off.
    3. Audio settings such as individual mic levels and master volume
      1. This rarely needs to be touched
8. When concluded with meeting, please Shut Down the computer to also shut down the room
  1. If room does not shut down when computer is powered off, tap touch panel Power menu, and Shut Down System to preserve projectors

# General Info

- 4 microphones on charger, each are labeled and one is labeled “Does not work”
- In-ceiling speakers in front of room are unplugged to prevent feedback
- To completely disable projector and screen, manually power off projector, and the screen with light switch on ceiling stage-right near screen with ladder or pole.
- For a more professional looking meeting, a nice camera and tripod can be requested from the design team (Steven Byeon)
- Windows behind presenter have a shade and blackout pulldown
  - Shade helps with bringing water in focus and overall lighting
    - Blackout to reduce distractions and variable lighting
    - See nested items below.
